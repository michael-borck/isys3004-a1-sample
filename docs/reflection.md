> **Note for students:** This is a placeholder showing what a reflection document should address. A real reflection would be 500–750 words written in your own voice about your actual experience building your portfolio. It should be specific — not "I learned a lot about CSS" but "I spent two hours trying to get the nav to collapse on mobile before I understood that I needed to toggle a class with JavaScript, not just hide it with CSS."

---

## What a real reflection covers

The spec asks you to address:

**1. The bridge between chats**  
What did you carry from your thinking chats into your build chats? For example: "In my Week 3 thinking chat I decided to use CSS custom properties after asking why they're better than hardcoding values — in the build chat I arrived knowing exactly what variables I needed, which made the brief much more specific."

**2. What surprised you**  
Something you didn't expect to learn. For example: "I assumed dark mode would require two separate stylesheets. Learning that changing a single attribute on `<html>` is enough — because CSS variables cascade down from there — was genuinely surprising."

**3. Honest assessment**  
What you fully understand vs what remains unclear. This is not a trap — markers want to see self-awareness. For example: "I understand how the theme toggle works end-to-end. I'm less clear on exactly why `async/await` works the way it does — I can use it correctly but I couldn't explain the JavaScript event loop from scratch."

---

## What makes a strong reflection

- Specific examples from your actual project (not generic statements)
- Honest about what you don't fully understand
- Shows a link between your thinking chats and your decisions
- Reads like it was written by you, about your actual experience

## What makes a weak reflection

- Generic statements that could apply to anyone ("I learned HTML, CSS, and JavaScript")
- No specific examples
- Reads like a summary of the assignment requirements
- Clearly written after the fact without reference to actual decisions made
